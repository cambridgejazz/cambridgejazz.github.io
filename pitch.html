The Cambridge Jazz Festival

25th – 29th November 2015

INTRODUCTION

  • The organisers of the Cambridge Jazz Festival are seeking funding of up to £15,000

  • The funding will be used to support festival costs including venue hire, musicians, ticketing, marketing and administration

  • The festival is to be held a range of venues across the universities and the city

  • Cambridge offers a unique location for a jazz event with iconic venues across the university and city and a strong connection with the arts

  • Sponsors will access a large base of approximately 30,000 students who are amongst the highest quality in the country,

  • There is a large overall population weighted towards younger people with high levels of education and interest in music and contemporary arts

  • The organisers (Cambridge Jazz Festival Community Interest Company) have successful launched two pilot events in Cambridge in 2014

  • The Cambridge Jazz weekends, held in June and November 2014 involved over 25 bands and workshops in 12 venues across the city

  • The festival is operated in partnership with university and city institutions including St John’s College, Clare College, Churchill College, Anglia Ruskin University and Cambridge Modern Jazz Club

THE FESTIVAL

  • The 2015 Cambridge Jazz Festival builds on the success of pilot events and will be held from 25th to 29th November 2015

  • It will comprise 5 days of concerts, educational workshops and school performances

  • There will be a headline concert on each day of the festival featuring mainstream national or international artists. Smaller scale events and workshops featuring regional and emerging artists will be built around the main events

  • The New York Standards Quartet has already been booked to perform on 26th November

FUNDING and BUDGETS

  • The Festival is supported by funds from St John’s College (Jazz @ Johns), Cambridge Modern Jazz Club, individual donations and sponsorship

  • The event is scalable and will depend of the final level of funding received

  • Funding is required to underwrite the costs of venue hire, artist fees, ticketing, marketing and general administrative costs

  • This will be offset by ticket sales with any surplus funds at the end of the event reinvested in the future festivals

  • Cambridge Jazz Festival Community Interest Company Limited is a registered community interest company established for the purposes of organising jazz festivals in Cambridge



SPONSORSHIP

  • Sponsors will be named prominently in marketing material including programmes, tickets, posters, social media, websites and in press releases

  • We will also offer festival naming rights to a lead sponsor subject to negotiation

  • The event offers an extremely low cost and effective sponsorship with the opportunity to reach a high quality audience of students and local community
